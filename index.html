Sustainability

Environmental and social policy

At Bonaveri, sustainability means taking responsibility every single day. It touches everything the company does, from the materials it selects to the way it collaborates with people, partners, and suppliers.

Renewable, responsibly sourced materials

FSC Chain of Custody certification

Redesigned sustainable packaging

On-site photovoltaic energy

Bonaveri operates a certified Environmental Management System (UNI EN ISO 14001), ensuring full compliance with environmental and safety regulations. This commitment extends across the entire value chain, with suppliers and partners encouraged to uphold the same high standards.

Sustainability at Bonaveri is not a destination but an ongoing journey.

A track record of sustainable innovation

Sustainability at Bonaveri is driven by research, innovation, and measurable results.

In 2016, Bonaveri introduced BNatural, the world's first mannequin made using BPlast (a sugarcane-based polymer) and finished with BPaint, a range of plant-based coatings. This innovation reduced CO₂ emissions by approximately 25% compared to conventional petrochemical mannequins.

BNatural mannequin

As the business evolved towards greater customisation and flexibility, these original materials became less practical for day-to-day production, prompting a new phase of research.

Bio-Resin Integration

Since 2019, Bonaveri has introduced an innovative bio-resin that is now fully integrated into production processes and used in approximately 20% of them.

Bio-Linen

A flax fibre-based composite certified OK Biobased (2 stars), currently being tested with a view to expanding its use across the range.

Bonaveri's commitment spans the full product lifecycle. Rental models promote the reuse of mannequins, while ongoing projects focus on end-of-life recovery and the development of circular economy solutions.

Redesigned packaging

Sustainable packaging

Packaging has been completely redesigned in collaboration with the University of Bologna, using FSC-certified materials, fully recyclable components, and optimised design to improve logistics efficiency while significantly reducing environmental impact. This work earned a CONAI Special Award in 2017 and a further mention in 2019.

Research and recognition

Much of this work has been underpinned by a long-standing partnership with the Politecnico di Milano, who conducted a full lifecycle assessment of Bonaveri's mannequins, mapping the environmental impact of every stage from design through to end of life.
